## Material categories

| Category | What it covers | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| Organics | herbs, resin, spores, scales, fur, feathers, claws | medicine, food, bait, some TMs |
| Mineral | ore, grit, crystal dust, salts, shell fragments | traps, gear, TM substrates |
| Tech Scrap | wire, lenses, batteries, springs, casings | tools, drones, jammers, blanks |
| Seam Reagents | warped dust, anomaly gel, echo shards | seam gear, restricted TMs |
| Refined Media | solvent, binders, plates, fibers, blank cartridges | advanced crafting and TM etching |

### Part-quality modifiers

| Part quality | MV modifier | Example |
|---|---:|---|
| ordinary part | +0 | shed fur, loose scale, common ore grit |
| specialized part | +1 | Mareep wool, Gligar claw, poison sac, horn fragment |
| pristine, refined, or rare sample | +2 | polished crystal lens, intact venom gland, perfect coil |
| warped or seam-touched | +2 | echo shard, seam resin, anomaly-soaked fiber |

## Type-alignment rules

- A harvested part is usually tagged to **one of the Pokemon's types**.
- For dual-type Pokemon, choose the type the party is harvesting toward.
- A single part cannot count as two types at once.
- **Normal-type materials** can count as a support material for **any non-Ghost TM**.
- Normal materials are excellent for stabilizing, padding, or widening the usable value of a craft.

## TM-specific support materials

| Support material | What it does |
|---|---|
| TM blank | required physical plate to etch a one-use TM |
| etching solvent | keeps the plate stable during inscription |
| binder resin | helps mixed-material TMs hold together |
| alignment lens | reduces bad rolls on high-grade TM work |
| Center archive access | substitutes for a field blueprint in many cases |

Practical recommendation:
- treat **TM blanks** as a bought, salvaged, or rewarded resource rather than something the party makes every time from scratch

## Fast conversion rule
If you do not want to track exact parts, convert route rewards into **MV bundles**.

| Bundle size | MV total | Example |
|---|---:|---|
| small bundle | 2 | a handful of herbs or one clean scrap part |
| standard bundle | 4 | one mid-stage part or two basic parts |
| strong bundle | 6 | one final-stage part or refined salvage |
| premium bundle | 8+ | apex salvage, warped core, prototype fragment |
