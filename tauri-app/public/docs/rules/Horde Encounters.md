# Horde Encounters

See also: [Main rules.md](Main%20rules.md), [DM Rules - Level Caps, Raids, Fusion, Defuse.md](DM%20Rules%20-%20Level%20Caps,%20Raids,%20Fusion,%20Defuse.md), [Crafting Recipes - Core Families.md](Crafting%20Recipes%20-%20Core%20Families.md)

## Core read
Use horde rules when the party is dealing with **many weak Pokemon at once**.

This is for:
- Zubat floods in caves
- Cutiefly or mosquito clouds in wetlands
- Rattata alley rushes
- stampedes of small herd Pokemon
- panic scenes where numbers matter more than any single combatant

Do not run a full stat block for every member of the horde.
That is slow and usually not the point.

Instead, run the horde as one pressure package with tags and a few tracked values.

## When to use horde rules instead of normal combat
Use a horde when most of these are true:
- there are `6+` Pokemon on one side
- most members are individually weak or disposable
- the danger comes from being surrounded, swarmed, blinded, or worn down
- the scene should feel fast and messy instead of tactical one-by-one cleanup

If there are only a few dangerous enemies, run normal combat instead.

## What a horde tracks
Each horde gets:
- a **size band**
- a **Pressure track**
- a **threat tag**
- an optional **core**

### Size bands
- **Small horde:** `5 to 8` Pokemon
- **Medium horde:** `9 to 15` Pokemon
- **Large horde:** `16 to 30` Pokemon
- **Overrun scene:** `30+` Pokemon or effectively endless waves

### Pressure track
Pressure is how dangerous the horde still is as a group.

Starting Pressure:
- Small horde: `3`
- Medium horde: `5`
- Large horde: `7`
- Overrun scene: `9` or use scene objectives instead of trying to clear it fully

When Pressure hits `0`, the horde is broken.

Broken means one of the following:
- it scatters
- it loses nerve
- enough members are driven off
- the party reaches safety
- the alpha or core has been neutralized and the rest peel away

## Threat tags
Give the horde one or two tags.

Common tags:
- **Swarm:** hard to pin down, likes chip damage and distraction
- **Stampede:** pushes people, knocks them prone, overruns space
- **Flying cloud:** ignores ground clutter, good at harassment
- **Venomous:** trades raw damage for poison or debuffs
- **Burrowing:** pops up from bad angles, threatens bags and footing
- **Territorial:** gets nastier if you stay in its zone
- **Scavenger:** goes for food, supplies, and weak targets
- **Noisy:** ruins stealth, concentration, and aim

## Optional core rule
Some hordes have a **core**:
- an alpha
- a queen
- a lure beacon
- a fused knot
- a territorial nest point

If the core is identified and dealt with, remove `2 Pressure` immediately.

Good core examples:
- the Golbat driving the cave swarm
- the Vespiquen holding a Combee cloud together
- the herd leader in a desert stampede
- the glowing fungus patch anchoring a spore horde

## Initiative and turns
Use your normal battle initiative or scene order.

The horde gets **one shared turn**.
Do not give every member a separate turn.

On its turn, the horde usually does one of these:
- rush someone
- blanket an area
- force movement
- inflict chip damage
- threaten supplies or escape routes

## What players do against a horde
Players should be allowed to fight it in more than one way.

Common actions:
- blast an area with AoE or crowd-control moves
- hold a chokepoint
- target the core
- scare it off with fire, noise, or a dominant Pokemon
- protect civilians or supplies while retreating
- split the mass with terrain or hazards

## Dealing Pressure damage to the horde
Successful player actions remove Pressure.

Default Pressure loss:
- single-target success against the mass: remove `1 Pressure` if it clearly thins or breaks formation
- strong area move or perfect chokepoint play: remove `2 Pressure`
- direct core takedown or scene-winning tactic: remove `2 to 3 Pressure`

Weak or partial success:
- hold the horde back for a moment
- negate its action this round
- no Pressure loss, but no penalty either

Failure:
- the horde keeps its Pressure and hits back

## What the horde does to the party
Instead of tracking lots of tiny attack rolls, let the horde inflict one package of pressure.

Pick one result on a successful horde action:
- chip HP damage
- chip SP loss
- loss of positioning
- obscured vision or bad footing
- grabbed bag, torn clothes, dropped item, or ruined prep
- forced save against poison, fear, or distraction

Suggested loss scale:
- Small horde: light chip, nuisance, or one bad status risk
- Medium horde: real chip plus forced movement or disruption
- Large horde: serious movement control, split party risk, and repeated chip pressure

## Horde behavior menu

### Swarm
The horde spreads around a target.

Effect:
- target suffers chip damage or distraction
- ranged accuracy and delicate actions get worse until the swarm is cleared or driven off

### Surround
The horde takes space away.

Effect:
- target must move, get boxed in, or risk worse penalties next round

### Stampede
The horde moves like a wall.

Effect:
- forced movement
- prone or collision risk
- civilians and supplies are endangered

### Screen
The horde makes it hard to see, aim, or reach the real threat.

Effect:
- concealment
- escape route blocked
- core harder to target until the screen is broken

### Scatter and reform
The horde breaks apart to dodge area punishment.

Effect:
- reduce the effect of the next obvious AoE
- but if the players predicted it, the horde can be trapped while reforming

## Catching Pokemon in a horde
Catching in the middle of a horde should be harder unless the party has already reduced Pressure.

Rule of thumb:
- at full Pressure, catching is difficult and risky
- at half Pressure or lower, targeted catching becomes reasonable
- at `0 Pressure`, one frightened or isolated Pokemon may be catchable by normal means

If the party wants a specific member:
- identify it first
- isolate it
- then attempt the catch

## Item and crafting hooks
These already fit the vault well:
- `Bug spray` reduces nuisance swarm pressure scenes
- `Insect hood` helps against eye-and-face harassment
- `Swarm Lure` can attract a horde on purpose
- `Smoke Capsule` can break line of sight and open a retreat lane

## Quick build templates

### Bat cloud in a cave
- Size: Medium
- Pressure: `5`
- Tags: Flying cloud, Noisy
- Core: Golbat screecher
- Usual threat: visibility loss, panic, split path

### Wetland insect wave
- Size: Large
- Pressure: `7`
- Tags: Swarm, Venomous
- Core: nest cluster or Vespiquen
- Usual threat: poison checks, ruined concentration, supply nuisance

### Desert herd burst
- Size: Medium
- Pressure: `5`
- Tags: Stampede, Territorial
- Core: lead bull or matriarch
- Usual threat: forced movement, trampling, dust blindness

### Alley scavenger pack
- Size: Small
- Pressure: `3`
- Tags: Scavenger, Noisy
- Core: bold alpha
- Usual threat: food theft, bag snatching, crowd mess

## DM guidance
Hordes should create decisions, not paperwork.

Ask:
- what is the horde trying to do
- what is the party trying to protect or reach
- what breaks the horde fastest: force, fear, terrain, or the core

If the answer is obvious, let the players solve it quickly.
If the answer is messy, make the horde a mobility and pressure problem instead of a bag of hit points.
