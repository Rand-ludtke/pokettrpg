# Crafting Quick Reference

## Core formulae

### Standard crafting
- roll `D12 + half Intelligence`
- compare against recipe DC

### TM crafting
1. add typed MV
2. `Starting BP Budget = typed MV x 10`
3. make Intelligence check
4. apply conversion percentage
5. round down to nearest 5 for final BP cap

## Conversion percentages

| Result | Percentage |
|---|---:|
| Fail by 5+ | 50% |
| Fail by 1-4 | 75% |
| Meet DC | 100% |
| Beat DC by 5+ | 125% |
| Natural 12 | 150% |

## Known recipe rule

- Trainers only know recipes that match their current **research rank unlocks** plus any blueprints, manuals, or story-taught recipes they have earned.
- Unlocking a family means those recipes can now be learned and crafted; it does not automatically mean every rare variant is free knowledge.
- TM work starts entering the known-recipe lane at **rank 6** with Tech Tools, but proper etching still needs better stations later.

## Travel craft pacing

- usual limit: **1 meaningful craft per short rest**
- usual long-rest limit: **2 quick crafts** or **1 meaningful craft + 1 quick craft**

## Medicine shorthand

- Pokemon medicine is the default answer for restoring Pokemon HP away from a Pokemon Center.
- Human medicine covers trainer HP, wound care, stimulants, and weather injury treatment.
- Field treatment without medicine uses the Main Rules recovery procedure and is weaker than real medicine.

## TM reminders
- blank required
- blueprint(either paper or have a pokemon who knows the move) or Pokemon Center required
- Pokemart blueprints cost 25% of the matching TM price, rounded up to the nearest 250
- crafted TMs are one-use
- gym reward TMs are multi-use
- Normal materials can support any non-Ghost TM
