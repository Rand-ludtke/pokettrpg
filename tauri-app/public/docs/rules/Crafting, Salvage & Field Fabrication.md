## What this system is for
This is the sequel campaign's **field-crafting, salvage, and fabrication layer**.

It gives the party useful reasons to:
- harvest route materials
- salvage Rocket, Suture, and wild-zone junk
- turn Pokemon parts into tools instead of only trophies
- make consumables, traps, gear, and one-use TMs in the field

This system should feel like **expedition logistics**, not spreadsheet torture.

## The seven crafting families

| Family | What it makes | Usual station |
|---|---|---|
| Medicine | Pokemon medicine, human medicine, salves, antidotes, revive-adjacent patches | kit, camp, Pokemon Center |
| Food | rations, buffs, bait, lures, recovery meals | camp, kitchen |
| Traps | smoke, nets, flash tools, adhesive devices | kit, camp |
| Expedition Gear | ropes, masks, route markers, weather gear | camp, workshop |
| Tech Tools | scanner pucks, jammers, batteries, drones | workshop, Pokemon Center |
| Seam / Fusion Gear | anchors, stabilizers, containment tags, barrier pucks | workshop, lab |
| TMs | one-use etched TM plates from typed source materials | Pokemon Center, lab, or field rig with blueprint |

## Core loop

### 1. Gather
The party collects raw materials from:
- routes and biomes
- Pokemon parts or sheddings
- urban scrap
- Rocket/Suture salvage
- warped-zone residue

### 2. Refine
Raw materials become components:
- herbs become tinctures
- ore becomes powder or plates
- fur becomes fiber bundles
- coils, lenses, and casings become tech parts
- seam residue becomes stabilized reagent

### 3. Match recipe or blueprint
- Standard crafting uses a recipe.
- TM crafting uses a move blueprint or Pokemon Center move archive access.

### 4. Make the check
Most crafting uses **Intelligence**.

Use the normal system from [Main rules.md](Main%20rules.md):
- `D12 + half Intelligence (round up)`

### 5. Resolve quality
- low success = reduced yield or rough quality
- strong success = better yield, stronger item, or cleaner build
- a natural 12 can create a premium version at DM discretion

## Stations and what they allow

| Station | What it can do | Limitations |
|---|---|---|
| Field Kit | very simple medicine, bait, low-tier lures, smoke, rope fixes, emergency trap work | no advanced tech, no seam gear, no TM etching |
| Campsite | food, medicine, basic gear, basic traps | no heavy fabrication |
| Workshop | traps, expedition gear, tech tools, most seam gear | needs time and power |
| Pokemon Center | advanced medicine, diagnostics, clean processing, common TM etching | rare TM work may still need a blueprint |
| Lab / Vulmotech / Gym backroom | premium tech, seam gear, prototype work, high-grade TM etching | access controlled by story or rank |

## Default craft DCs

| Complexity | DC | Typical use |
|---|---:|---|
| Simple | 10 | common medicine, meals, marker kits, smoke balls |
| Standard | 12 | stronger medicine, bait, good traps, rope gear |
| Advanced | 15 | scanners, stabilizers, drones, strong TMs |
| Restricted | 18 | prototype gear, rare seam tools, elite TMs |

### Result bands

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Fail by 5+ | wasted time; lose half the materials |
| Fail by 1-4 | rough build; item works at reduced effect or yield |
| Meet DC | normal item |
| Beat DC by 5+ | improved item, extra charge, or one bonus rider |
| Natural 12 | premium result if the recipe reasonably allows it |

## Default time cost

| Item scale | Time |
|---|---|
| quick item | 10 to 15 minutes |
| standard item | 30 minutes |
| advanced item | 1 to 2 hours |
| prototype / restricted item | half day or full downtime scene |

Party rule:
- During travel, allow **1 meaningful craft per short rest** or **2 quick crafts per long rest** without bogging the session down.

## Material value
Most recipes use **Material Value (MV)**.

MV comes from:
- evolution stage
- rarity of the part
- whether the part is warped, refined, or unusually pure

Use [Crafting Materials Index.md](Crafting%20Materials%20Index.md) for the actual charts.

---

## Practical GM rule
- If a player has a cool, logical ingredient idea, let the part count if the typing and stage make sense.
- If the scene is about pacing, reduce counting and just convert the haul into MV bundles.
- If the scene is about scavenging a specific mon or route, use exact parts.

The system should create **choices and flavor**, not paperwork for its own sake.
